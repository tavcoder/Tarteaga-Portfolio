Verycheck, your verified information

A few weeks ago, I participated in the COVID-19CHALLENGE organized by Uxer School, where, alongside 7 other professionals, we voluntarily and remotely worked on providing a solution to the uncertainty caused by the excess of information that generates distrust in the data we receive.

Based on the Design Thinking methodology, Verycheck emerged. Conceived as an extension, it tracks news already verified by certified organizations. Through an intuitive interface resembling a traffic light, it indicates the accuracy status of the information.

PROBLEM

People often seek more information when significant social events arise, but the sheer volume frequently leads to confusion and distrust. There is a need to access verified news and data, but how facilitated access to transparent and verified information ?

UNDERSTAND

Surveys and interviews

We took our first step by launching a survey to clarify how users accessed information and were informed. The survey results indicated that individuals most informed about the current pandemic were workers whose employment had been affected by COVID-19. Therefore, these were the profiles chosen for the interviews.

KEY AREAS OF FOCUS:

In the current situation, how often do people stay informed?

Through which channels?

What type of information interests them the most?

What are their primary sources of information?

How concerned are they about the accuracy of information?

FINDINGS

There is no consensus on what constitutes a reliable source. They prefer consulting official sources even when these are incomprehensible.

Accessing information about assistance is not easy; it is neither centralized nor complete.

They miss verified data.

USERS

Using quantitative and qualitative data from the interviews, we defined the two profiles of the target group: Rafael (Self-employed, 39 years old) and Raquel (Affected by ERTE, 43 years old), aiming to empathize better with the main user groups and prioritize objectives according to their needs.

DEFINE AND IDEATE

Rephrasing the interview findings into How Might We (HMW) questions, we aimed to turn every challenge into a design opportunity.

Focusing on these three questions, we defined the following ultimate challenge to guide our solutions:

How could we improve the accuracy of information and access to it for individuals whose employment is affected by COVID-19?

DECIDE

We categorized potential solutions, and we analyzed and put to a vote those identified as Innovative and Feasible. With the selected idea, we conducted a brainstorming session and defined our value proposition:

Our online verification service aids users who seek information on the internet and aim to access accurate information, reducing distrust and obtaining transparent, verified data.

PROTOTYPING

We created a prototype of the product landing page to showcase the idea and its functionality to the user. Subsequently, we tested whether the user would install it. Similarly, we prototyped how the news would appear after installing the product to verify the effectiveness and visibility of the seal.

Chrome Extension

This extension seamlessly integrates with the Google search engine, enabling you to check the credibility of content on different websites as you browse.

Integration with social networks

Verifying messages with URLs from the IFCN database is crucial for ensuring information accuracy.

TESTING

We validated the prototype through moderated user testing, assigning tasks to users. They performed these tasks while speaking aloud about their actions and thoughts during interviews. We obtained the following results from these tests:

• The traffic light system is highly intuitive and serves as a universal code.
• Not knowing who is verifying the information creates distrust.
• The classification of yellow-labeled news as partially false is incomprehensible.


In light of these conclusions, we finalized the concept
and decided that Verycheck would function as a metaverifier, collecting verified news and misinformation from specialized
websites (Maldita, Newtral, Chequeado, etc.) and providing the information in centralized manner.

We also included the verifying source's seal in the verifier's presentation
