OBI,
48h challenge

To practice our skills and reminisce about our university days, some friends and I decided to participate in the UX challenge launched by Reply Challenge in Germany. We had a 48-hour window to propose a design alternative that tackle a real-world issue.

GOALS

Create an alternative to the OBI DIY application within 48 hours for both current and new users. Based on the company's prior research, this new proposal aims to provide:

• A guarantee of success: The customer will not fail in their effort if they use our services and embark on their DIY projects.

• Support when needed: We are there to assist them at every step of the way, bringing their DIY dreams to life by:

    • Inspiring them with great ideas;

    • Providing guidance through numerous tutorials and tips on how to do it;

    • Offering advice from certified experts;

    • Providing excellent prices on top brands, products, and high-quality materials.

OBI

OBI is a German hardware store that currently operates 530 stores in 13 countries. Unlike other hardware stores, their business model relies on users assembling furniture themselves by selling kits with instructions and necessary materials. Additionally, they provide workshop rental services in their stores

Understanding users' weaknesses to devise how to provide them with a guarantee of success.

Why do people fail in DIY projects? Well, maybe they don't have the tools or the budget. They fear investing time in something that won't yield a satisfactory result. They have doubts at some point in the project and can't proceed confidently. Or maybe they're just scared because they lack experience. We need to ensure that we address these fears with our proposal. Here's how we did it:

Show projects based on experience: People feel more confident when things align with their preferences and skills. That's why we decided that initially, each user would be categorized as: Potential Talent, Expert, and DIY Master based on their experience. Each project will display the required experience level so that people can choose those that suit them best, complete them successfully, and boost their confidence.

Confidence in every step: How can you be sure you'll finish the project if you don't know how to complete the next step? For users with doubts, we've created the option to upload a photo of the current step and get online assistance.

Others have done it too! In addition to details, each DIY project will showcase images of how others have already completed it. (These images will be taken from photos users upload while finishing their projects).

Everything you need: Not only will you be able to access DIY projects, but you can also buy or rent any tools you need. And if you don't have space in your home to do it, you can reserve your spot at the nearest OBI workshop!"
